Embrace

Embrace presents a woman who had surrendered. She is looking skyward, falling backwards, and has her arms open- in an embrace, in surrender, and in acceptance to whatever life throws at her. The fall is into a void of colors and mystery that has no end or beginning. By surrendering to ones vulnerable and raw emotions and by reaching a patch of darkness, you are able to find comfort and a way out. When you embrace your emotions and thoughts, you are able to find a new path and perspective, which allows you to leave anew and victorious.

2019. Acrylic painting.







Beyond The Depths

The hardships that we face throughout life have a purpose. The word ‘Strength’ and the lion invoke the stability and durability an individual builds for themselves that leads to happiness and joyous experiences in life. When you rotate the image, you see the journey undergone to obtain said happiness. Through this lens, an individual reaches out, looking skyward from the depths of the ocean, surrounded by darkness and cruel words that haunt the depths of her mind. But the closer that one gets to the surface, the more light there is. Given time, we are able to leave the lonely and dark depths. The ugly parts in life are unavoidable. By exiting them, we are able to strengthen ourselves and become happier. The orange figure symbolizes happiness, which one obtains through having strength (red) and hope (yellow). Live without fear and unapologetically swim towards the surface.

2020. Mixed Media (Screen printing, acrylic paint, and embroidery).

BLMxPride

In the wake of the strengthening of the Black Lives Matter protest from May and June of 2020, I wanted to create a graphic to show my solidarity with the Black community as well as one that would serve as a reminder to those who are not Black, that Black people are important and equal to us all. Black people have paved the way for many, if not all of us. This piece features Marsha P. Johnson in the center, a gay American liberation activist and drag queen. Marsha P. Johnson was an outspoken advocate for gay rights and a prominent figure in the LBGBTQ+ community and of the Stonewall Uprising of 1969.

2020. Adobe Illustrator & Photoshop.

Decision

As someone who has always been interested in criminology, thrillers, and murder mysteries, this was a huge inspiration and driving force behind this piece. This game revolves around you, the player, who has to decide what actions to take after witnessing a woman’s motorcycle being stolen. One thing leads to another, which leads you to make an ultimate decision- one of life and death. I wanted to give the user a fun and interactive game that would keep them on their toes and engaged throughout. Along with the thrilling plot, I decided to visually enforce the game with background images that correlate to the location of each passage (which I got from Google Images and edited in Adobe Illustrator). While the subject of the game is a bit heavy and dark, the aesthetic and visuals are bright and light, creating an intended contrast and irony. Thee Backyard Fairyscape

Being stuck at home for 8 monthes, there weren't many places I could escape to or new places for me to see or explore. One thing that has captivated me during this time at home has been the sky. It changes every hour of the day, is infinite, and can convey different moods. This piece captures how the sky, and nature in general, can have a serene effect on us a mortal and finite beings. The gradients, color scheme, and soft brush strokes add to the magical and fictional aura the piece exudes as a way to put the viewer into a calming, far away, and dream induced haze.

2020. Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop.







The Me

The person and individual that you act is in front of others does not always reflect the true you. Many times, we bring forth a fake identity- whether it be in real life or on social media. The mask of not showing your true self will begin to surround you and proceed to swallow you up until you break (as represented through the flowers). The Me is a piece that shows what a person presents is not always what they are really going through or feeling. One can be beautiful and happy on the outside and still end up self destructing and being left a broken mess. This piece serves as a reminder to be kind to others because you never know what they are internally feeling and that we should all take care of our mental states and accept and acknowledge the emotions we feel.

2020. Adobe Photoshop.

Lay with Me Beneath the Gemini Sky

Dreams. We all have them no matter how big or small. Dreams are vast, boundless, beautiful, and mysterious- just like space. Being heavily based upon stargazing, this surreal landscape transports the viewer to the middle of a plane surrounded by mountains under a cloudy blue night sky. The scene is accompanied by striking constellations and a red moon while ambient music and the sound of whirring wind plays in the background, providing a visual and auditory experience for the viewer. The title “Lay with Me Beneath the Gemini Sky” derives from the fact that the moon phase and constellations shown are based on those present on my birthday (May 23, 2002), with Gemini season falling from May 21 to June 20. This piece serves as an invitation to the viewer to pursue and immerse themselves in their dreams and goals and to bask in the beauty of their potential, imagination, and efforts. From there, you can also access the virtal reality installation of "Lay with Me Beneath the Gemin Sky".
